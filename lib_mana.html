University Library Management System – Abstraction and Information Hiding

Abstract Entities

Book
Represents each item in the library’s collection.
Student
Represents library users who search, borrow, and return books.
Librarian
Represents the staff who manage the library and its operations.
BorrowRecord
Tracks borrowing transactions, linking books and students with dates and fine details.

Use of Abstraction

  • Book abstraction: When students search for books, only key info like title, author, and category is provided. Details like publisher, edition, and ISBN are hidden from students for simplicity.
  • Student abstraction: Students see their own names, borrowed book list, and due notifications, but do not see others' fines or full histories.
  • Librarian abstraction: Only necessary details (name, duties) are visible to the public. Their credentials and authority are kept out of view.

Information Hiding for Sensitive Data

  • Librarian credentials: Accessible only to the relevant authorized individual, never shown to other users.
  • Student fines: Only the student concerned and librarians can view or modify fines. Fines remain hidden from other students and the public.
  • BorrowRecord privacy: Fine details and borrowing history access is strictly restricted to authorized roles only.

Examples of Concepts

  • A student searches for “Data Science” books. They see the list of matching titles and authors, but not the ISBNs or publisher details.
  • The librarian logs in (using hidden credentials) and removes outdated books, adds new arrivals, or updates fines for overdue books.
  • Only the librarian and the concerned student can see fine details or pay/resolve fines via a secure interface.

Summary Table: Abstraction and Information Hiding

Entity Exposed Details (Abstracted) Hidden Details (Information Hiding)
Book Title, Author, Category Publisher, Edition, ISBN
Student Name, Borrowed books, Due notifications Fines, Full borrowing history
Librarian Name, Duties Login credentials, Permissions
